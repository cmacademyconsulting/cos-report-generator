Our Process — Fully Transparent

AI-assisted automation.
CCM human review. Always.

We believe you deserve to know exactly how your compliance report is produced. This transparency is part of the COS™ methodology — and it is what makes our service trustworthy.

What the automation does

The COS™ Report Generator structures your project data against the requirements of publicly available international frameworks — including the ADB Safeguard Policy Statement, IFC Performance Standards, ISO 45001, and GRI Standards. Each report maps findings to the specific clauses of these frameworks. These are public documents; CM Academy references them as published standards. CM Academy is not an endorsed or approved partner of any issuing organisation. Reports prepared by a CCM apply the CMAA Body of Knowledge in professional practice.

What the CCM review does

Every report is personally reviewed by Susil Bhandari, CCM (CMAA-USA) — 22 years of field experience across Nepal, Qatar, and Bahrain — before it reaches you. The review checks that findings are accurate, scores are justified, and the report reflects what is actually happening on site. The CCM review is what makes the report trustworthy — and professionally accountable.

Why this benefits you

A traditional compliance consultant charges $5,000–$15,000 and takes 3 weeks. They also use templates and standard frameworks — just without telling you. Our approach is rigorous in process, transparent in method, and delivered in 24 hours at a sustainable fee that makes mandatory reporting accessible to every project — not just large ones.

Our ethical commitment

The COS™ Methodology is open-access and published on Zenodo (DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.18802971). Every score in every report is derived from data you provide — not from what any authority wants to see. A report that returns 44% on Oversight is delivered as 44%. We do not adjust scores for approval. That is the difference between compliance reporting and compliance theatre.

Ethics First — Always

The standard we hold ourselves to.

These principles are not a marketing statement. They are the operating rules of CM Academy and the COS™ Methodology. They are published here so the world can hold us to them.

Principle in Practice · 01
"A report that lies to protect a project kills the worker it was meant to protect."
Compliance documentation that reflects desired outcomes rather than site reality is not compliance — it is risk transferred silently onto the people with the least power to protect themselves. Every COS™ score is derived from data. Every finding is reported as found.
Principle in Practice · 02
"Credentials are earned. Endorsements are granted. Neither can be borrowed."
Professional credibility is built one verifiable claim at a time. CM Academy states only what has been formally conferred — and nothing more. We do not imply institutional approval we do not have. We do not claim a credential before the issuing body authorises it. This discipline is not modesty. It is the foundation of trust.
Principle in Practice · 03
"Transparency is not a feature of ethical practice. It is the definition of it."
Every client of CM Academy is told exactly how their report is produced — what the AI-assisted system does, what the CCM review does, and what the client's own data determines. There is no hidden process. There is no method we would be embarrassed to explain. If we would not publish it, we do not do it.
Principle in Practice · 04
"The construction site is where ethics is tested — not in the office where the report is written."
Twenty-two years of field experience across Nepal, Qatar, and Bahrain — watching compliance reports filed on projects where workers were still getting hurt. The COS™ Methodology was built to close that gap. Not to make the paperwork look better. To make the site safer.
Principle in Practice · 05
"Knowledge that costs nothing to share costs everything to withhold."
The COS™ Methodology is published open-access on Zenodo under CC BY 4.0. Any engineer, student, contractor, or government agency anywhere in the world can study, apply, and build on it freely. Commercial services sustain the practice. The knowledge belongs to society.
Principle in Practice · 06
"An evergreen professional grows from ethics. A seasonal one wilts without clients."
Professional reputation built on honest credentials, transparent process, and real results compounds over decades. CM Academy exists to build that kind of practice — not one dependent on favourable conditions, low scrutiny, or clients who do not ask questions. We welcome scrutiny. We invite it.
The COS™ Code of Ethics

Seven principles.
Non-negotiable. Always.

These principles govern every report produced, every claim made, every credential displayed, and every relationship CM Academy holds with clients, institutions, and the public. They are derived from the CMAA Code of Ethics, the COS™ Methodology White Paper (DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.18802971), and 22 years of field practice.

1
Truth Before Approval
Every COS™ report reflects what is actually happening on site. Scores are derived from data provided by the project team — not from what any donor, authority, or client wants to see. A project that scores 41% on Oversight is delivered as 41%. Adjusting scores to secure approval, satisfy a client, or avoid a difficult conversation is a violation of this code and grounds for immediate termination of service.
2
Credential Integrity
No credential is claimed before it is formally conferred by the issuing body. No organisational endorsement is implied without written authorisation from that organisation. Every qualification stated publicly is exactly what has been earned and verified — nothing more, nothing less. This applies to every certificate, every badge, every professional title, and every institutional affiliation displayed on any CM Academy platform.
3
Transparent Process
Every client is informed clearly and in advance how their report is produced — including the role of AI-assisted structured generation and the role of CCM human professional review. No process is concealed. No method is misrepresented. The transparency section of every CM Academy communication exists not to manage perception but to give clients the information they need to make an informed decision.
4
Worker First
The ultimate beneficiary of every COS™ compliance report is not the donor, the PMC, the contractor, or the executing agency — it is the worker on site. One construction worker dies every ten minutes globally, often on projects with active compliance documentation. COS™ reports are produced to protect real people in real conditions. Any engagement that asks CM Academy to prioritise approval over worker safety is declined.
5
Accurate Attribution
International standards and frameworks — ADB Safeguard Policy Statement, IFC Performance Standards, ISO 45001, GRI Standards, CMAA Body of Knowledge — are referenced as the publicly available documents they are. CM Academy is not an endorsed, approved, or certified partner of any issuing organisation. These frameworks are referenced because they represent the best available professional consensus. Attribution is always accurate, always honest, and always complete.
6
Sustainable Before Scalable
CM Academy does not promise services it cannot yet deliver, claim markets it has not yet served, or project income it has not yet earned. Each phase of this practice is built only after the previous phase is generating honest, sustainable results. This discipline protects clients from overpromising, protects the profession from inflated expectations, and protects the COS™ brand from the credibility loss that follows when ambition outpaces execution.
7
Open Access for Society
The COS™ Methodology is published permanently and freely on Zenodo (DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.18802971) under Creative Commons CC BY 4.0. Any person, institution, government, or organisation anywhere in the world may study, apply, adapt, and build upon it without cost or permission. Commercial compliance report services fund the practice. The knowledge behind them belongs to the profession and to society. That is not a business model. It is a responsibility.
